4. Uncle Toms Cabin

Uncle Toms's Cabin is number 4 on our list. Uncle Toms' Cabin was a book written by Harriet Beecher Stowe which stirred up even more controversy between the North and the South. The publishing of this book also diminished British sympathy for the southern people even more. This is number 4 on the list because it really raised northern morale and strengthened northern abolitionism, while on the other hand, it diminished southern hope.
