3. John Brown's Raid on Harpers Ferry/Hanging of John Brown

In 1859 John Brown and a group of people went to Harpers Ferry, Virginia to start a raid on a federal armory. Their goal was to start an armed revolt of slaves and destroy the institution of slavery. Sadly, their attempt failed and John Brown and six of his soldiers were tried for treason. Weeks after the raid John Brown was hung. It had a big impact because it raised the tension for the presidential election in 1860 and when he died, many people were inspired by him and his cause to try and get rid of/protest against slavery. His raid greatly angered the south because they took it as an attack from the North against them. The effects of the raid and his death are a huge factor in the cause of the Civil War because it greatly separated the South from the North.
